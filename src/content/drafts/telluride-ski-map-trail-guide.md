## Resort Overview and Layout

Telluride Ski Resort spans two distinct base areas connected by the free gondola, creating a unique layout that requires understanding to navigate effectively. The resort's terrain extends from Mountain Village (9,545 feet) to the summit of Palmyra Peak (13,150 feet), with the historic town of Telluride (8,750 feet) serving as the lower base area. Understanding this layout helps skiers plan routes, choose appropriate terrain, and maximize time on the mountain.

**Mountain Village Base Area:** Located at 9,545 feet, Mountain Village serves as the primary base area with most ski-in/ski-out accommodations, restaurants, and resort services. The area provides access to the main lift system and serves as the hub for most skiing activities. The free gondola connects Mountain Village to downtown Telluride, providing easy access to both areas.

**Telluride Base Area:** Located at 8,750 feet in the historic town, Telluride provides a secondary base area with gondola access to Mountain Village and the main lift system. The area offers a different atmosphere with historic charm and walkable access to restaurants and shops. The gondola provides 13-minute transportation between the two base areas.

**Terrain Distribution:** Telluride's 2,000+ acres are distributed across multiple areas: Mountain Village base area, Prospect Bowl, See Forever, Gold Hill, and Palmyra Peak. Each area offers distinct terrain characteristics, from gentle beginner slopes to challenging expert terrain. Understanding terrain distribution helps skiers choose areas matching their ability levels and interests.

## Lift System Overview

Telluride's lift system includes 18 lifts serving terrain across the resort, from beginner-friendly magic carpets to high-speed lifts accessing expert terrain. Understanding the lift system helps skiers navigate efficiently and access desired terrain.

**Primary Lifts:**
- **Village Express (Chair 4):** High-speed quad serving Mountain Village base area, accessing intermediate and advanced terrain
- **Oak Street (Chair 8):** High-speed quad accessing Prospect Bowl and intermediate terrain
- **See Forever (Chair 9):** High-speed quad accessing See Forever area and advanced terrain
- **Gold Hill (Chair 10):** Fixed-grip quad accessing Gold Hill expert terrain
- **Revelation (Chair 15):** High-speed quad accessing Palmyra Peak and expert terrain

**Beginner Lifts:**
- **Meadows (Chair 7):** Fixed-grip triple serving dedicated beginner terrain
- **Magic Carpets:** Multiple surface lifts in beginner learning areas

**Gondola System:** The free gondola connects Mountain Village and Telluride, providing transportation and access to mid-mountain terrain. The gondola operates from 7:00 AM to midnight during ski season, providing convenient access between base areas.

## Terrain Breakdown by Difficulty

Telluride's terrain is distributed across difficulty levels, with approximately 23% beginner, 36% intermediate, 41% advanced/expert terrain. Understanding terrain distribution helps skiers choose appropriate areas and plan routes matching their ability levels.

**Beginner Terrain (23%):** Located primarily in the Meadows learning area and lower mountain areas, beginner terrain provides gentle slopes perfect for first-time skiers and those learning. The Meadows area is isolated from faster traffic, creating safe learning environments. Magic carpets eliminate chairlift challenges for beginners, while gentle slopes allow skill development without intimidation.

**Intermediate Terrain (36%):** Distributed across multiple areas, intermediate terrain provides varied challenges for developing skiers. Prospect Bowl offers wide-open intermediate runs, while See Forever provides intermediate terrain with stunning views. Intermediate terrain connects well, allowing skiers to explore multiple areas while staying within ability-appropriate terrain.

**Advanced Terrain (41%):** Telluride's advanced and expert terrain provides challenges for experienced skiers, with steep pitches, moguls, glades, and backcountry-style terrain. Gold Hill offers expert terrain with challenging pitches, while Palmyra Peak provides advanced terrain with stunning views. Advanced terrain requires strong skills and appropriate equipment.

## Trail Difficulty Ratings

Understanding Telluride's trail difficulty ratings helps skiers choose appropriate terrain and navigate safely. Ratings follow standard North American classifications: green circles (beginner), blue squares (intermediate), black diamonds (advanced), and double black diamonds (expert).

**Green Circles (Beginner):** Gentle slopes with wide trails, perfect for first-time skiers and those learning. Beginner trails are well-groomed and isolated from faster traffic, creating safe learning environments. The Meadows area provides dedicated beginner terrain with magic carpet access.

**Blue Squares (Intermediate):** Moderate slopes with varied pitches, suitable for skiers with basic skills. Intermediate trails provide opportunities to develop skills while enjoying varied terrain. Prospect Bowl and See Forever offer excellent intermediate terrain with good variety.

**Black Diamonds (Advanced):** Steep slopes requiring strong skills and experience. Advanced trails may include moguls, narrow sections, or challenging pitches. Gold Hill and Palmyra Peak offer advanced terrain for experienced skiers.

**Double Black Diamonds (Expert):** Extremely challenging terrain requiring expert skills, appropriate equipment, and mountain awareness. Expert terrain may include steep pitches, moguls, glades, or backcountry-style conditions. Some expert terrain requires avalanche safety equipment and knowledge.

## Navigation Tips

**Understanding the Map:** Telluride's ski map shows terrain distribution, lift locations, and trail connections. Study the map before skiing to understand layout and plan routes. The map shows difficulty ratings, helping skiers choose appropriate terrain.

**Using the Gondola:** The free gondola connects Mountain Village and Telluride, providing transportation and mid-mountain access. Use the gondola to move between base areas or access mid-mountain terrain. The gondola operates from 7:00 AM to midnight, providing convenient access.

**Terrain Selection:** Choose terrain matching your ability level, starting with easier terrain and progressing as skills develop. Telluride's terrain distribution allows skiers to find appropriate challenges at all ability levels. Don't ski terrain beyond your ability level.

**Group Navigation:** When skiing in groups with different ability levels, use "meet at the bottom" strategies where skiers take different routes but regroup at lift bases. Designate meeting points and compromise terrain that works for all group members.

**Mountain Awareness:** Be aware of your location, trail difficulty, and conditions. Telluride's terrain can be challenging, and mountain awareness prevents problems. Know your limits and ski within them.
