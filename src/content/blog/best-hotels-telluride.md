# 15 Best Hotels in Telluride: Top Places to Stay (2025)

Telluride offers accommodations ranging from historic Victorian-era boutique hotels on Main Street to ultra-luxury ski-in/ski-out properties in Mountain Village. After analyzing guest reviews, inspecting properties, and considering factors like location, amenities, value, and ski access, we've identified the 15 best hotels in Telluride across all categories and price points.

Whether you're seeking slope-side convenience, downtown nightlife access, family-friendly amenities, or budget-conscious value, this guide helps you find your perfect Telluride basecamp.

**Important Note:** Hotel ratings, prices, and amenities are subject to change. Price ranges reflect typical rates but vary significantly by season, room type, and availability. Always verify current rates and availability directly with hotels or booking platforms before making reservations.

For help deciding between downtown and Mountain Village, see our [where to stay guide](/blog/hotel-reviews/where-to-stay-telluride). To plan your timing, check our [best time to visit](/blog/planning-tips/best-time-visit-telluride).

## Booking Tips

**Book Early:**
- 6-9 months for Christmas/New Year's
- 3-4 months for peak season (January-March)
- 1-2 months for shoulder season

**Compare Direct Booking:**
- Hotel websites often match or beat third-party sites
- Direct booking may include perks (breakfast, upgrades, parking)

**Ask About Packages:**
- Ski/Stay packages often include lift tickets
- Spa packages save 15-20% at resort hotels
- Multi-night stays may include dining credits

**Consider Location Trade-offs:**
- Mountain Village: Ski convenience, higher prices, less atmosphere
- Downtown: Better value, dining/nightlife, 10-minute gondola to slopes

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the best hotel for first-time visitors?**
Hotel Telluride offers the best balance of location, value, and amenities for newcomers. Downtown access lets you experience Telluride's character while still being close to skiing.

**Which hotels are truly ski-in/ski-out?**
The Madeline, Peaks Resort, Lumiere, Inn at Lost Creek, and Capella offer genuine ski-in/ski-out access in Mountain Village.

**What's the most affordable hotel in Telluride?**
Oak Street Inn and Camel's Garden Hotel offer the lowest rates ($100-150/night in shoulder season), though expect basic accommodations.

**Do I need to rent a car if I stay in Telluride?**
No. The free gondola connects downtown and Mountain Village, and both areas have free shuttles. A car is only useful for exploring surrounding areas.

**Which hotels are best for families with young children?**
Peaks Resort excels with kids' programs, multiple pools, and ski-in/ski-out convenience. Lumiere works well for larger families needing space.
